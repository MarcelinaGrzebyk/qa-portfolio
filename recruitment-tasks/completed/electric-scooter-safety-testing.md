## Electric Scooter Safety Testing

Imagine you are employed by a company that manufactures electric scooters and are responsible for ensuring the safety of a new product. Please outline the safety tests you would conduct.

Depending on my position and responsibilities (quality controller of the finished product or controller of algorithms and operating systems), following the rules of the Agile system, I would proceed with my work as follows:

First, I would list all the functionalities of the final product.

Then, taking into account the risk assessments, I would decide which functionalities are most important in terms of safety of use. To eliminate the risk of dangerous situations, I would rank the features from the most important in terms of safety to any additional features that have minimal or no impact on the safe use of the product. In the initial phase of testing, I would place the greatest focus on testing the correct functioning of the key features of the product.

## Safety Tests

I would include the following tests:

- Proper operation of the controller and engine (expected result for throttle movement)

- Proper operation of brakes (expected results taking into account smoothness at different speeds, ABS and CBS system operation)

- Battery condition check - acceptable temperature range (expected behavior in case of exceeding or falling below the acceptable range)

- Battery charge check (expected result in case of charging completion or discharge, e.g., while driving)

- Lighting (proper operation of lights and turn signals)

- Power unlocking with a key or PIN code (wheel lock, sound alarm as expected result in negative tests, immobilizer)

- Parking stability - (Are the mechanical components enabling parking solidly constructed and capable of supporting the weight of the device, and correct operation of the “Tip-Over detection”)

## End-to-End (E2E) Testing

To perform E2E testing for the finished product, I would list all additional functionalities in a similar scheme, such as:

- all product actions controlled by the application (GPS location, battery level control, remote power blocking, etc.)

- correct display of battery level and navigation map on the scooter display, engine power recovery function (recuperation)

- as well as functionalities affecting riding comfort – cruise control, heated grips, parking sensors and air-conditioned seat
